🟢 Slide — Use case diagram
Script (~50s)

"This is the use case diagram of the platform. The big rectangle is the system itself — the Adaptive Learning Platform — and everything outside it is an actor.
I have two primary actors, who are humans: the Student, the main user, and the Administrator. And one secondary actor: OpenAI, an external AI service that the system calls.
The Student can authenticate, take a placement test, follow a personalized path, consult lessons and videos, take a quiz, ask the AI tutor, and view their progress. The Administrator can manage the content, the users and the AI models, and view the statistics.
The dashed link from OpenAI to Ask the AI tutor means the system relies on OpenAI only for that specific use case — to generate the explanation, which is then verified."
Why we did it this way (if the jury asks)

Why a secondary actor? In UML, an actor is always outside the system. OpenAI is an external cloud service → secondary actor. Ollama, SymPy and the databases are internal, so they are not actors — they belong in the architecture.
Why this split of use cases? Each use case maps to a functional requirement we already identified — the diagram simply links each one to the actor that triggers it. It stays consistent with the "actors" and "functional requirements" slides.
Why the dashed line for OpenAI? To show a partial dependency: the system depends on OpenAI only to generate the tutor's answer, not for anything else.
